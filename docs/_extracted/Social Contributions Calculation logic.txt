Technical Specification – Social Contributions Calculation (Simulation 2026)
1. Engine Objective
This engine calculates an estimated social contribution for a self-employed person based on an estimated net income provided by the user.
It does not reproduce the full administrative logic of Belgian social insurance funds, such as:
provisional contributions
N-3 income basis
later adjustments (regularization)
Instead, it provides a direct estimation based on the 2026 contribution rates.
The goal is to minimize the risk of future regularization.
Social contributions are paid on a quarterly basis.
2. Definition of Estimated Net Income
The estimated net income corresponds to the estimated annual professional income after deducting professional expenses.
Formula
estimated_net_income = estimated_professional_income - estimated_professional_expenses
Example
Estimated income: €80,000
Estimated expenses: €30,000
Estimated net income: €50,000

5. Management Fees Calculation
After calculating the legal contribution, the social insurance fund management fees must be added.
Partena Management Fee Rate
4.20 %
Formula
total_contribution = legal_contribution × 1.042
6. Engine Calculation Flow
retrieve social_status
retrieve estimated_professional_income
retrieve estimated_professional_expenses
calculate estimated_net_income
apply rate table according to status
apply minimum contribution if applicable
calculate legal_quarterly_contribution
add management fees (4.20 %)
return total_quarterly_contribution
7. Engine Pseudo Code
def calculate_contribution(status, net_income): legal_contribution = calculate_rate(status, net_income) total_contribution = legal_contribution * 1.042 return total_contribution
