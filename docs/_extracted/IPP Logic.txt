IPP Calculation:
Taxation is done by tax brackets:
Each person is entitled to a tax-free allowance of €10,910. This means that this part of the income is not taxed within the income tax brackets.
This allowance may increase in certain cases (dependent children / age / marital status / etc.).
Example:
Jean lives in Belgium, is single, and has no children or other dependents. He earns a taxable income of €38,000.
His basic tax is calculated as follows:
First, Jean’s income is divided into brackets, in accordance with the table above. The corresponding tax rate is then applied to each income bracket:
In the first bracket, Jean earns €16,320 (€16,320 - €0). A rate of 25% is applied to this bracket. Jean therefore has to pay €4,080 tax in the first bracket.
In the second bracket, Jean earns €12,480 (€28,800 - €16,320). A rate of 40% is applied to this bracket. Jean therefore has to pay €4,992 tax in the second bracket.
In the third bracket, Jean earns €9,200 (€38,000 - €28,800). A rate of 45% is applied to this bracket. Jean therefore has to pay €4,140 tax in the third bracket.
We obtain the total tax by adding the amounts from the different brackets:
€4,080 + €4,992 + €4,140 = €13,212
It now remains to take into account the tax-free allowance.
To do this, we first calculate the amount of tax Jean would in principle have to pay on the amount of income covered by the tax-free allowance.
For Jean, the tax-free portion of income amounts to €10,910. For this amount, the tax rate is 25%. Jean is therefore entitled to a tax exemption of €2,727.50 (25% of €10,910).
We subtract this exempt amount from the total tax calculated in step 2:
€13,212 - €2,727.50 = €10,484.50
Jean therefore has to pay €10,484.50 in tax.

Married or legally cohabiting — tax impact:
The “marital quotient” allows up to 30% (max. €13,050 for 2024 / income 2025) of the professional income of the higher-earning spouse to be fictitiously allocated to the other spouse, reducing the household’s total tax burden. The goal is to reduce tax pressure, especially if one of the partners does not work or works little.
Example:
Married couple / legally cohabiting partners
• Spouse A: €60,000• Spouse B: €0
Deduction of the employee professional expenses lump sum: €5,930
60,000 – 5,930 = 54,070
Transfer calculation → 30% of 54,070 = €16,221, but note the maximum is €13,050
A = 54,070 – 13,050 = 41,020B = 13,050
Why this is advantageous: the transferred income from A is taxed less because it reduces A’s taxable base, therefore a lower tax bracket applies. The other spouse here is taxed at 25%.
