1. Calculation Architecture of the Taxes Module
The module must be designed around 3 engines:
1. IPP Engine – Individual
Used for the self-employed individual and for the company director as a natural person.
2. ISOC Engine – Company
Used to estimate corporate income tax on net taxable profit.
3. Bridge Engine – Company ↔ Director
Used to connect:
• the director’s remuneration,• dividends,• social contributions possibly paid by the company,• benefits in kind,• and their simultaneous impact on both ISOC and IPP.
This is exactly the overall logic of the document:
if Self-employed → IPP + Social Engine;if Company → ISOC Engine, then possibly IPP Director Engine + Bridge Engine.
Fiscal Profile Questions (Taxes…)
Core backend structures to plan for

3. Fundamental rule: calculation order
For the engine to remain stable, the order must be the following.
Individual
Identify the tax and social regime.
Identify the activity period.
Estimate net professional income.
Add other professional or salaried income.
Determine estimated social contributions.
Determine net taxable income.
Apply family parameters.
Calculate IPP.
Apply municipal surcharges.
Deduct withholding taxes and withholdings.
Deduct or compare advance tax payments.
Display estimated final balance.
Company
Determine the fiscal year.
Determine net taxable profit.
Determine the applicable ISOC rate.
Calculate corporate tax.
Determine remaining cash.
Integrate flows to directors.
Calculate the director’s IPP.
Consolidate the global view.
The SPF Finances confirms that corporate income tax is calculated on the company’s net income and that the standard rate is 25%.

Questions:

Calculation logic
Fields:
• salaried_income_taxable• salary_withholding_tax_amount
Professional withholding tax is a tax withheld at source on remuneration, and it reduces the final tax due.
Calculation rule
Salaried income
professional_income_salary_component = salaried_income_taxable
Credited taxes
credited_withholding_tax += salary_withholding_tax_amount
Global impact
Salaried income increases the professional income base:
total_professional_income_before_social = salaried_income_taxable + self_employed_profit + other_professional_income
Then:
• gross IPP is calculated on total income;• withholding tax is deducted from the balance.
Final balance
estimated_balance_due = total_tax_due - credited_withholding_tax - advance_payments
“I don’t know” case
If the user does not know the withholding tax:
• store salary_withholding_tax_known = false• do not inject any credit• display a warning: estimate may be overstated
Screen 12 — Estimated Self-Employed Profit
Choices:
• Manual estimation• Automatic extrapolation• Conservative simulation• I don’t know yet
Calculation logic
Fields:
• estimated_profit_mode• estimated_annual_net_profit
1. Manual estimation
estimated_annual_net_profit = entered_amount
2. Automatic extrapolation
The engine must use available to-date data.
Generic example:
estimated_annual_net_profit = current_net_profit / elapsed_months * 12
or, if no YTD profit is available, use a base from another module.
3. Conservative simulation
Use either:
• a prudent low assumption,• or a safety coefficient.
Example:
estimated_annual_net_profit = auto_extrapolated_profit * conservative_factor
where conservative_factor may be configurable, for example 0.85 or 0.9 depending on your product.
4. I don’t know yet
Two possible options:
• force a minimal, non-committal simulation;• or display a partial result without a reliable final IPP.
Critical dev point
The engine must always distinguish:
• estimated_profit_source = manual / extrapolated / conservative / unknown
because the front end will need to display a reliability level.

Screen 15 — Other Income
Cases:

Calculation logic

General rule
Each category must be stored separately.Do not put everything into one single “other income amount”.
Recommended structure

Why
Because taxation differs depending on the nature of the income.
Dividends

Foreign income
It may affect the tax base, exemption with progressivity, or other mechanisms depending on treaties.
Other professional income
It must be added to the total professional base.

global_income = professional_income + rental_component + foreign_component + alimony_component + other_component
But in the backend it is necessary to distinguish:
• income to be globalized,• income subject to separate taxation,• income already subject to withholding.
Product recommendation
Even if the screen does not yet request amounts today, the backend must be ready to receive them.

15. The most important point for developers
They must understand that each question belongs to one of these 4 categories:
1. Routing question
It selects a journey.
2. Tax parameterization question
It loads a rate, a rule, or a table.
3. Monetary question
It directly enters a formula.
4. Consistency question
It secures the quality of the calculation, even if it does not directly enter the formula.
This is exactly how this module must be developed, otherwise they risk:
• mixing routing and calculation;• hardcoding rates;• losing the distinction between estimate and certain amount;• making the module impossible to maintain.
